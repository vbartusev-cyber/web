Strategy JustificationsWhy each quadrant
Manage Closely — High Power / High Interest
Active Collaboration
These stakeholders hold regulatory, financial, or operational authority over the project and stand to gain or lose significantly from its outcome. NERSA can revoke a licence; the Treasury controls budget; Eskom controls grid connection. Monthly steering committee meetings are mandatory.
Keep Satisfied — High Power / Low Interest
Proactive Reporting
Funding institutions and investors hold financial power but are not operationally involved. Their primary concern is ESG compliance, loan repayment security, and risk visibility. Quarterly impact reports and risk dashboards keep them aligned.
Keep Informed — Low Power / High Interest
Transparent Communication
Operations staff, local communities, and field engineers are directly affected by day-to-day project activity. Though they hold limited formal power, their collective resistance could delay work. Bi-weekly toolbox talks and town halls prevent surprises.
Monitor — Low Power / Low Interest
Lightweight Oversight
Equipment suppliers and public housing associations have a peripheral relationship with the project. Disproportionate engagement would waste project manager bandwidth. Annual reviews suffice unless their circumstances change.
